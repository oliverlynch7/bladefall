## Premise
You play **the last Warden** — the final survivor of an order sworn to descend beneath the
world and keep an ancient evil, the **Abyss King**, asleep. For centuries his nightmares have
seeped up through the rock, rotting the land and "loosening the laws that hold a body down"
(the in-world reason you can jump/fly). The Wardens descended in shifts to keep him under.
**None ever came back up.** You took the same oath; you are the only one left to keep it.
The game is a **descent** through eight worsening zones to the sleeper — and then past him.

## Recurring character: the Warden's Shade
At the mouth of each zone you meet the **Shade of a dead predecessor** — a Warden who died at
that post and still mans it. Each gives lore, explains why the zone's quests matter, and warns
you about the boss. Same doomed order, one dead voice per zone, each further down and more lost.

## Structure (8 zones, descending)
1. **The Outskirts** — "Where the road ends and the wild begins." Boss: The Brute
2. **Hollow Pass** — dry canyons. Boss: Hollow Marksman
3. **Ruined Keep** — a fortress the dead never left. Boss: The Warden (a fallen predecessor)
4. **Frostfell** — cold deep enough to still the blood. Boss: Frost Sorcerer
5. **Emberdeep** — the mountain's furnace. Boss: Ember Colossus
6. **The Abyss** — "Where the world thins and something looks back." Boss: the Abyss King (the sleeper)
7. **The Sunspire Palace** — white marble the long war never reached. Boss: The Marble Colossus
8. **Castle Duskmoor** — the last gate; ascend the towers to the throne room. Final boss: The Void Tyrant

**Known plot seam to smooth:** story-wise the Abyss King (Zone 6) reads as the climactic
"sleeper," yet two more zones and the true final boss (the Void Tyrant) come after. Palace and
Castle are currently framed as places the war "never reached" / "the last gate." Tightening the
connection between the Abyss King and the Void Tyrant is the single biggest opportunity.

## The Warden's Shade — per zone (lore / why quests matter / boss warning, verbatim)

**Outskirts**
- Lore: "I held this road for eleven years. Now I only haunt it." / "The dreaming spreads through everything that lives here — cut it back, or it follows you down."
- Why: "Thin the grunts and light the old waystone. Every marker you wake weakens his reach up here."
- Boss: "The Brute at the wood's end was a farmer's ox. Do not let what it has become reach you."

**Hollow**
- Lore: "The wind you hear is the canyon trying to empty itself." / "We posted watchers on the rims. The rims are why you'll live or die here."
- Why: "The chargers breed in the nests — burn through them. The bones on the rims were our watchers. Bring them down for burial."
- Boss: "The Marksman was our best archer. She still holds her post at the head of the pass. She will not miss twice."

**Keep**
- Lore: "This keep was ours. The order that failed you built its failures a prison here." / "What walks these halls wears armor you'd recognize."
- Why: "Destroy the risen and recover the sigils — each one is an oath the dead are still bound by."
- Boss: "The thing in the deep cell was a Warden. He descended further than any of us. Kill him like you'd want to be killed."

**Frost**
- Lore: "Cold this old isn't weather. It's the dream refusing to let anything move on." / "We lost the Frostwatch in a single night."
- Why: "The frostlings carry cores of the old cold. Shatter them, and find the cairn we never finished raising."
- Boss: "The Sorcerer came here to study the ice. Around the fourth winter, it started studying him back."

**Ember**
- Lore: "The mountain's furnace should have died with the mountain. The nightmare keeps it fed." / "Everything down here wants to be lava when it grows up."
- Why: "The emberlings shed shards of the core-fire. Quench them, and mark the ash altar so the way stays open behind you."
- Boss: "The Colossus is not guarding the deep. It IS the deep. Bring it down joint by joint."

**Abyss**
- Lore: "You are below every post we ever held. Even my death doesn't reach much deeper." / "The dark here isn't empty. It's attentive."
- Why: "Banish what you can and take back the splinters — every shard of the rift you carry out is one he can't dream through."
- Boss: "Past this point sleeps the King. He has been listening to you descend the whole way."

**Palace**
- Lore: "I never held this post. None of us did. The Sunspire kept itself." / "The marble here remembers being carved. Do not turn your back on any of it."
- Why: "Put down the risen guard and gather the gilded relics — the throne was hoarding them, and each one is a piece of the way forward."
- Boss: "The Colossus is the throne's own statue. It has had centuries to practice standing up. End it before it finishes."

**Castle**
- Lore: "This is the last gate, Warden. Past the towers there is only him." / "There's nothing left to teach you. Whatever you've become on the way down — be exactly that."
- Why: "Clear the towers as you climb. Every shadeling you cut is one less thing at your back when you reach the top."
- Boss: "Break the cycle at the summit. Or become the next thing I have to warn somebody about."

## Ending (after the Void Tyrant falls, verbatim)
- The Void Tyrant comes apart, and the world exhales.
- The stone remembers itself. Far above, a waystone catches.
- But the Abyss is patient, and an oath holds only while someone lives to keep it.
- You climb out. You are still the last Warden.
- The cycle is broken. Cycles are rebuilt.

- Palace: shatter 18 Marble Sentinels, find the Throne Antechamber, gather 5 Gilded Relics
