# BLADEFALL — Harsh per-zone level critique + fix plan (against the 9 principles)

> Critique grounded in each zone's actual SCAPE source (layout, den placement, landmarks,
> objectives) — not vibes. Ranked worst→best. The 9 principles are the bar. Follow `AGENTS.md`.

## THE 4 SYSTEMIC FAILURES (what's wrong across the board)
**A. Combat on cramped / moving / edge footing (breaks #6 & #7 — the worst).** The traversal
set-pieces (ramparts, causeways, floating shards, swaying bridges, spire climbs) force fights
on narrow, moving, or gap-edged ground. For a DODGE-ROLL game this is fatal: you roll off an
edge and die to *geometry*, not to a mistake. The dodge is the core verb and the levels punish
using it.
**B. Directionless layouts (breaks #1 & #9).** Outskirts (a loose loop of clearings) and the
Abyss (floating shards + a phasing causeway) have no readable single golden path — a first-run
player wanders, unsure which way is forward or where the goal is.
**C. Pacing is placement, not a curve (breaks #5).** Every zone is "3 roughly-even dens along
the path." There's no warm-up → rising → breather → boss RHYTHM, no deliberate quiet-then-loud
waves, no pre-boss breather. Combat never lands hard because it never builds.
**D. Inconsistent destination wayfinding (breaks #2).** Palace and Hollow show their goal from
the start; Outskirts and others don't. Not every zone has a horizon landmark pulling you forward.

## RANKED CRITIQUE + FIX (worst first)

### 8th / WORST — THE OUTSKIRTS  (grade: D — and it's the FIRST zone, so this matters most)
**Source:** "wide open grass clearings joined in a loose LOOP + spur"; 3 thornboar dens across
clearings C1/C3/C4; find-shrine on a knoll; secret in a hidden glade off the far west.
**Critique:** the make-or-break FIRST impression is the most **directionless** zone in the
game. A loose loop of six same-height clearings has NO golden path (#1) and no forward vector
(#9) — a new player literally doesn't know which clearing is "next" or where the exit is.
Pacing is flat: three near-identical boar fights in a ring (#5). Pretty meadow, zero guidance.
**Fix:**
- Impose a **golden path**: make ONE route through the clearings visibly the main line (wider,
  a lit/worn trail), the loop demoted to optional exploration.
- Put the **find-shrine/waystone knoll on a rise that's VISIBLE from spawn** as the destination
  landmark (#2), with the exit portal beyond it, glowing.
- **Escalate** the three dens: a lone boar (warm-up) → a boar+sporeback pack (rising) → a bigger
  fight by the goal (#5), with quiet meadow between each.
- Keep the open, gentle feel — just make "forward" unmistakable. First zone = most legible, not
  most wandering.

### 7th — THE ABYSS  (grade: D — worst combat footing in the game)
**Source:** floating shards; "shadelings HOLD THE SHARDS" (dens on shore, west shard, ruin);
fork = phasing causeway vs a drift path across SWAYING islands (movers).
**Critique:** the floating-world fantasy directly **fights the dodge mechanic** (#6). Combat on
small floating shards ringed by LETHAL void gaps means a dodge-roll off the edge = instant
death by geometry, not skill. The phasing causeway (floor that appears/disappears) and swaying
islands are the worst possible combat floors. And the floating/phasing route is the least
readable golden path in the game (#1/#9).
**Fix:**
- Combat shards become **big, flat, edge-SAFE arenas** — widen them and rim them with a low
  deco lip / broken railing so a roll doesn't fall off. Fights only happen on these.
- The **phasing causeway + swaying islands become pure TRAVERSAL** (no required enemies) — keep
  the tension of crossing, remove the combat-on-them.
- **Light the golden path** clearly across the floating geometry so the route reads (#2).

### 6th — RUINED KEEP  (grade: C — combat on a moving ledge over a pit)
**Source:** fortress split by a chasm; cross HIGH on ramparts (with a SWAYING broken span
mover) or LOW on a phasing causeway; "sentinels guard the ramparts."
**Critique:** fighting sentinels on the **narrow high rampart walk with a swaying gap over a
chasm** is a textbook #6 violation — the exact worst place for a dodge game. The two-route
concept is good, but combat belongs in neither the moving span nor the phasing causeway.
**Fix:**
- **Sentinel/rampart combat moves into the open baileys** (the two courtyards are the arenas).
- The **high rampart + swaying span and the low phasing causeway become pure traversal** — a
  tense crossing, but enemy-free. Reach the far bailey, THEN fight in the open.

### 5th — EMBERDEEP  (grade: C+ — combat on a switchback causeway)
**Source:** caldera descent; fork = Vent Gauntlet vs Spire Climb (6 pillar-hops); dens at rim,
**mid-causeway**, sanctum.
**Critique:** the **mid-causeway den** puts a fight on a narrow switchback causeway (#6), and
the Spire Climb fork is 6 cramped pillar-hops. Good descent structure otherwise.
**Fix:**
- Widen the **mid-causeway den into a proper vent-chamber ARENA** (an open landing off the
  causeway) with a retreat route + a pillar for cover from the flame-caster (#7).
- The **Spire Climb stays enemy-free traversal**. Keep the rim + sanctum fights (already open).

### 4th — CASTLE DUSKMOOR  (grade: C+ — risk of fights on swaying bridges)
**Source:** siege ascent; fork (rampart run / vented road) rejoining at the tower base; the
Broken Tower (spiral of hanging steps) → Last Bridge (two SWAYING spans); "the King's guard
hold EVERY STEP of the way"; dens at breach, courtyard, low road.
**Critique:** the main dens are open (breach/courtyard/low-road), which is good — but "guard
hold every step" + the spiral hanging steps + the two swaying bridge spans risk incidental
combat on the worst footing (#6) as the finale.
**Fix:**
- Keep the three arena dens (breach/courtyard/tower-base). Make the **Broken Tower spiral and
  the two swaying spans PURE traversal** — the climactic tension is the *climb*, not a fight on
  a swaying plank. Put the final guard stand in the **open Keep-Door arena** at the top.

### 3rd — FROSTFELL  (grade: B- — mostly solid)
**Source:** ascending glacier journey; fork = drifting floe-line vs the shore road; Crystal
Gallery interior; dens at camp edge + both ice sheets; frozen-Warden landmarks; aurora + cairn.
**Critique:** good journey, good landmarks, dens on the (flat) ice sheets are open-ish. The
one risk: the **drifting floe-line** — if combat happens on moving floes, that's #6.
**Fix:**
- Confirm no required combat on the drifting floes (make the floe-line a traversal fork, fights
  on the solid sheets). Add the pacing curve + a pre-boss breather (#5). Otherwise strong.

### 2nd — HOLLOW PASS  (grade: B — the STRUCTURE model)
**Source:** canyon journey; entry arch → corridor → rejoining FORK (loop) → Mesa checkpoint →
chasm → pillar field → Summit shrine; dens on the GROUND; bones front-loaded low; arch/cairn/
bone-pile/skull-arch landmarks.
**Critique:** genuinely good — clear golden path, great landmarks, combat kept on open ground,
verticality reserved for traversal + the secret, objectives front-loaded. This is the template.
**Fix (minor):** the three dustjackal dens feel same-y — **escalate them** and add a **pre-boss
breather** (a quiet checkpoint before the summit). That's it.

### 1st / BEST — SUNSPIRE PALACE  (grade: B+ — the WAYFINDING model)
**Source:** a straight ceremonial AXIS; Sun Court → colonnade with **god-ray light shafts** →
wings (intact west / corrupted-checkerboard east = the secret) → Grand Stair → Throne dais;
dens on the open axis; sentinels + a holy caster mid-hall.
**Critique:** the best zone. The straight axis is a perfect golden path (#1), the colonnade +
god-rays are textbook framing + light wayfinding (#2/#3), combat is on the open axis (#6/#7),
and the dangerous crumbling checkerboard is reserved for the optional secret. Nearly ideal.
**Fix (minor):** confirm the throne is the visible destination from the entrance; add a pre-boss
breather. Use this zone as the reference for the others.

## THE UNIVERSAL PACING TEMPLATE (apply to every zone)
1. **Warm-up:** quiet entry, learn the biome, one easy foe.
2. **Rising:** 2–3 combat beats of increasing pressure, each in an OPEN arena, separated by a
   quiet traversal/exploration/fork stretch.
3. **Breather:** a safe checkpoint before the boss (loot cache / vista / lull).
4. **Climax:** the open boss arena.
Combat ONLY on open, flat, edge-safe ground. Verticality / movers / gaps / phasing = traversal
and secrets ONLY. Every zone shows its destination landmark from the entrance and lights its
golden path.

## ✅ LOCKED BUILD DECISIONS (Oliver's QA — build to these)
1. **Combat footing — enforce, but KEEP A FEW.** Pull required combat onto open, flat,
   edge-safe arenas across all zones — EXCEPT keep **at most 2 deliberate set-piece fights**
   on dramatic cramped/edge geometry as intentional tension. Rules for the kept ones: they
   must be *chosen* (the most cinematic moments), *telegraphed* (the player sees the risk
   coming), and made *fairer than accidental* (widen the surface a bit / add a low rim so a
   single dodge doesn't equal death). **Recommended keepers:** (a) Castle Duskmoor's **Last
   Bridge** — a climactic guard stand on the swaying span to the Keep Door, and (b) Ruined
   Keep's **broken-span rampart** — one mid-game tension fight. Everything else = open arenas,
   traversal set-pieces enemy-free.
2. **Scope — ALL 8 zones in one pass.**
3. **The Outskirts — KEEP THE OPEN MEADOW.** Do not restructure it. Add: one obvious lit/worn
   main path threading the clearings (loop demoted to optional), the find-shrine/waystone knoll
   on a rise **visible from spawn** as the destination landmark with the glowing portal beyond,
   and escalate the 3 boar dens (lone → pack → bigger). Gentle feel stays; "forward" becomes
   unmistakable.
4. **The Abyss — BIGGER RETHINK of the combat spaces.** Keep the floating-world *look*, but
   substantially redesign where fights happen: replace the tiny-shard combat with a few LARGE,
   solid, edge-safe combat islands/arenas amid the floating fantasy; the phasing causeway,
   swaying islands, and small shards become pure TRAVERSAL between them; light a clearly
   readable golden path across the floating geometry. This is the one zone free to change its
   combat layout meaningfully, not just widen.

Apply the **universal pacing template** and the **9-principle checklist** (from
`LEVEL_DESIGN_PRINCIPLES.md`) to every zone. Hollow Pass and Sunspire Palace are the
references — bring the other six up to their bar.
