# BLADEFALL — Story
**Date:** 2026-07-15 · **Status:** draft for Oliver's review · **Scope approved:** intro cutscene + per-zone narration card + boss line + ending
**Built on:** the premise Isaac wrote for BLADEFALL 2D (Abyss King's nightmares, the last Warden, "break the cycle"). Oliver confirmed Isaac is fine with us building on it. Prose here is fresh; the spine is his.

## The premise in one paragraph
For centuries the **Abyss King** slept beneath the world, and his dreaming did the damage his waking never could. His nightmares seeped up through the rock — rotting the woods, souring the passes, and **bending the laws that hold things down**. Where the nightmare runs deepest, stone forgets to be solid and gravity forgets its job. An order called the **Wardens** existed to keep him asleep: they descended in shifts, they maintained the waystones, they held the line. The order is gone. The waystones are dark. You are the last one who ever took the oath, and the only question left is whether you go down.

## Why this premise earns its keep
Every line does mechanical work — that's why it's worth keeping:
- **"Twisted the very laws of gravity"** → this is why BLADEFALL is a *platformer*. The parkour isn't genre furniture, it's the corruption itself. It also retroactively explains Phase 4's hazards: **Phasing** is stone forgetting to exist, **Rime** is friction failing, **Gales** are pressure gone wrong, **Emberfall** is the world's heat escaping. The nightmare gets literally stronger as you descend, which is why the hazards do too.
- **"You are the last Warden"** → gives the hub a reason to be a hub (see below) and makes the class picker a story beat.
- **"Break the cycle"** → maps onto the difficulty ladder. Normal is the descent. Hardcore is the descent with no second chance. Hitless is the descent as the Wardens were *supposed* to do it: untouched.

## The load-bearing twist: The Warden
**Our tier-3 boss is named The Warden.** That is not a collision to paper over — it's the best beat available.

The thing chained in the Ruined Keep is **one of your predecessors**. A Warden who descended, got far, and did not break the cycle — he was *kept*. The Keep isn't a prison the enemy built; it's where the order's own failures ended up, and something down there is still feeding them. You fight your own dead order on the way down, and he is stronger than you because he got further than you have.

This reframes the whole first half: you're not exploring a dungeon, you're **walking the route your order already died on**, past their markers, their gear, and eventually one of them.

## How the systems become story
| System | Story |
|---|---|
| **The hub** | The Wardens' last waystation. The trainer, the wardrobe, the quest board and the empty portal row aren't menus — they're the headquarters of a dead order, still stocked, waiting for a shift that never came back. The shop's plain weapons are what's left in the armoury. |
| **Classes** | **Warden is a title, not a fighting style.** Warrior / Ranger / Mage is your *discipline* — how you were trained to hold the line. This is why the picker has a trial: you don't declare a class, you demonstrate one. |
| **The Reaper** | What a Warden *becomes* if they descend far enough and don't break. Not a monster and not a hero. That's why his gate is hidden in The Abyss and why the scythe has to be earned — you're proving you can walk that line without falling off it. |
| **Waystones (find quests)** | The order's markers. Lighting one is re-establishing a link that has been dark for a very long time. |
| **Gather quests** | What the previous shifts left behind. Tangleroot, Keep Sigils, Frozen Cores — supplies, insignia, and remains. |
| **Elites** | Nightmare that has been awake long enough to get organised. |
| **Side zones** | Places the nightmare pooled. Deeper, richer, and off the order's official route — which is exactly why the good gear is still there. Nobody was assigned to them. |
| **The bank / hub save** | The oath: what you carry out is yours, what you leave down there stays down there. Dying doesn't erase you — it just means the descent didn't count. |
